Barack Obama is an American politician, attorney, and author who served as the 44th President of the United States from 2009 to 2017. He is a member of the Democratic Party and was the first African American to hold the office.




Early Life & Education Born August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii. Attended Occidental College, then transferred to Columbia University, earning a degree in political science. Later attended Harvard Law School, where he became the first Black president of the Harvard Law Review. Political Career Worked as a community organizer in Chicago. Served as an Illinois State Senator (1997–2004). Served as a U.S. Senator for Illinois (2005–2008).


Presidency (2009–2017) Key achievements include: Passing the Affordable Care Act (“Obamacare”) Authorizing the operation that killed Osama bin Laden Supporting and legalizing same-sex marriage nationwide (via Supreme Court appointment influence and policy stance) Handling the 2008 financial crisis recovery Negotiating the Iran nuclear deal Expanding environmental regulations and clean-energy programs He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2009.
